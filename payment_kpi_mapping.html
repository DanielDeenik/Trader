The First Principle: What Is a Payment Company?

Strip away every brand, every product name, every earnings call buzzword. A payment company does exactly one thing: it moves money from point A to point B and takes a cut. Every KPI that matters is a derivative of three variables:

Volume

How much money flows through the pipe? Measured as TPV (Total Payment Volume), GMV, or processing volume. This is the raw throughput.

Take Rate

What percentage does the company keep? Interchange fees, processing fees, subscription fees. Revenue = Volume × Take Rate. This is the margin multiplier.

Network Size

How many endpoints (merchants + consumers) are connected? More endpoints = more volume = network effects. This is the moat.

Every other KPI is a leading indicator, quality indicator, or decomposition of these three. Cross-border volume? That's a high-take-rate subset of Volume. Active accounts? That's a proxy for Network Size. Chargeback rate? That's a quality tax on Take Rate.

The Camillo framework says alpha lives in the gap between what alternative data shows and what the market price reflects. Here are the specific KPI divergences that LATTICE's 12 connectors can detect:

The Knowledge Graph Edge These Divergences Create

Each divergence becomes a DIVERGES_FROM edge in the graph connecting two Metric nodes (e.g., "traffic_growth: +5.2%" and "price_return: −41%"). Over time, the graph accumulates divergence history. When the same divergence type persists for 4+ weeks, LATTICE should escalate it from "watch" to "high conviction" — this is the Gold Rush transitioning from "emerging" to "validating."
